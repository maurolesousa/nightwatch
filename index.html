Who We Are

Wellington's period
early music ensemble

The Night Watch performs on historical instruments — viola da gamba, baroque violin, lute, theorbo, harpsichord and recorders — as close as possible to those that sounded in the courts, churches and salons of 16th and 17th century Europe.

The ensemble collaborates with Australasia's finest early music specialists and visiting international artists, performing sacred and secular repertoire from plainchant through to the early Baroque.

Support Us

Why Your Support
Matters

Our model prioritises artistic integrity and audience experience over scale. We choose intimate venues. We keep ticket prices accessible. We engage specialist musicians from across New Zealand and Australia. This means our production costs will always exceed box office revenue.

Your support ensures that:

  • • historically informed Baroque performance thrives in New Zealand
  • • mid-career professional musicians sustain and deepen their artistic practice
  • • audiences experience world-class performance in deeply human settings
  • • rare and emotionally powerful repertoire continues to be rediscovered and heard

Without private investment, this level of artistic ambition is not possible.
